9.1 QOZB Overview

Operating Business Overview

Qualified Opportunity Zone Businesses (QOZBs) provide access to operating company investments beyond traditional real estate, offering portfolio diversification and potentially superior returns through business growth and operational improvements.

Qualification Requirements

QOZBs must derive at least 50% of gross income from active conduct of trade or business within the OZ, maintain substantial use of tangible property within the zone, and satisfy specific asset and income tests. These requirements necessitate careful structuring and ongoing compliance monitoring.

Attractive Sectors

• Technology and Software Development
• Advanced Manufacturing and Assembly
• Healthcare and Life Sciences
• Professional Services and Financial Technology
• Logistics and Distribution Operations

Investment Structures

QOZB investments can be structured as equity investments in qualifying businesses, acquisition and improvement of operating companies, or formation of new businesses within designated zones. Each structure offers distinct risk-return characteristics and compliance requirements.

Value Creation Strategies

Successful QOZB investments focus on businesses with scalable models, defensible competitive positions, and growth potential enhanced by OZ location advantages. The combination of tax benefits and operational improvements can generate exceptional returns for sophisticated investors.
