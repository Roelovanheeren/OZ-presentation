8.1 Metropolitan Overview

Metropolitan Overview

The Phoenix metropolitan area represents one of the nation's premier Opportunity Zone markets, combining rapid population growth, economic diversification, pro-business regulatory environment, and 149 designated zones across diverse submarkets and asset classes.

149 Designated Opportunity Zones

Demographic Fundamentals

Phoenix leads the nation in population growth with over 100,000 annual net new residents, driven by domestic migration from high-cost coastal markets. The metropolitan area benefits from favorable age demographics, strong household formation, and robust labor force expansion.

Economic Diversification

The regional economy has successfully diversified beyond traditional industries, establishing leadership in technology, advanced manufacturing, financial services, and logistics. Major corporate relocations and expansions have created sustained job growth and wage appreciation.

Infrastructure Development

Significant public and private infrastructure investment supports continued growth, including transportation improvements, utility expansion, and institutional development. The Arizona State University presence provides research capabilities and talent pipeline for knowledge-based industries.

Regulatory Environment

Arizona maintains business-friendly policies including competitive tax rates, streamlined permitting processes, and pro-growth development policies. The regulatory environment supports entrepreneurship and capital formation while maintaining quality of life standards.
