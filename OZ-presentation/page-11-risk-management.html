10. Risk Management

Comprehensive Risk Framework

OZ investments present unique risk profiles combining traditional investment risks with regulatory, compliance, and tax-specific considerations. Effective risk management requires sophisticated understanding of all risk vectors and appropriate mitigation strategies.

Regulatory Risks

Compliance Failure: Loss of QOF status or QOZP qualification.

Legislative Changes: Modifications to OZ tax benefits or requirements.

Mitigation: Professional compliance monitoring and legal counsel.

Market Risks

Economic Cycles: Real estate and business performance volatility.

Interest Rate Risk: Impact on valuations and financing costs.

Mitigation: Geographic and asset class diversification.

Liquidity Considerations

The 10-year holding period requirement creates significant liquidity constraints that must be carefully managed through appropriate portfolio construction, investor communication, and capital planning. Limited partnership structures should include appropriate liquidity provisions.

Operational Risk Management

Development and operating risks require experienced management teams, comprehensive insurance coverage, contingency planning, and active asset management. Sponsor track records and operational capabilities are critical success factors.
