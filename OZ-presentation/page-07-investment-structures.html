6.1 Investment Universe

Investment Universe Overview

Opportunity Zone investments span diverse asset classes and investment strategies, each with distinct risk-return profiles, compliance requirements, and operational considerations. The optimal asset class selection depends on investor objectives, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

Real Estate Development

Ground-Up Construction: Maximum compliance flexibility with original use property status.

Risk Profile: Higher development and execution risk offset by superior tax benefits and return potential.

Timeline: 24-48 month development cycles aligned with OZ holding period requirements.

Real Estate Acquisition

Value-Add Repositioning: Existing properties requiring substantial improvement investment.

Compliance: Must satisfy substantial improvement test with investment equal to acquisition cost.

Strategy: Repositioning, renovation, and modernization of income-producing assets.

Operating Business Investments

Qualified Opportunity Zone Businesses (QOZBs) provide access to operating company investments with potential for exceptional growth and job creation. These investments require sophisticated due diligence and active management but offer diversification beyond real estate.

Asset Class Optimization

Leading QOFs employ diversified strategies across multiple asset classes to optimize risk-adjusted returns while maintaining regulatory compliance. The combination of real estate and operating businesses provides portfolio diversification and multiple value creation pathways.
