5. Compliance Framework

Regulatory Architecture

The OZ compliance framework requires sophisticated operational infrastructure to maintain ongoing eligibility and maximize tax benefits. The regulatory requirements span entity formation, investment tracking, reporting obligations, and asset management protocols.

QOF Certification Requirements

Qualified Opportunity Funds must be organized for the purpose of investing in QOZ property and must satisfy the 90% asset test on the last day of the first 6-month period of the QOF's tax year and on the last day of the QOF's tax year.

Critical Compliance Deadlines

• 180 days: Maximum time from capital gain recognition to QOF investment
• 12 months: Working capital safe harbor for deployment
• 30 months: Substantial improvement completion deadline
• Annual: 90% asset test compliance verification

Asset Test Management

The 90% asset test requires continuous monitoring and active portfolio management to maintain compliance. QOFs must develop sophisticated tracking systems and operational procedures to ensure ongoing qualification while optimizing investment deployment.

Documentation Standards

Comprehensive documentation protocols are essential for maintaining compliance and supporting tax positions. This includes investment tracking, valuation procedures, compliance monitoring, and audit preparation systems.
