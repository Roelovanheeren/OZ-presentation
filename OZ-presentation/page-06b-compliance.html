5.3 Best Practices

Professional Infrastructure

Effective compliance requires coordination between specialized tax counsel, accounting firms with OZ expertise, experienced fund administrators, and qualified custodians familiar with regulatory requirements.

Reporting Obligations

QOFs must file Form 8996 annually to certify compliance with investment standards and asset tests. Investors must file Form 8997 to elect deferral and report their QOF investments. Proper documentation and record-keeping are essential for supporting these filings.

Audit Preparedness

The high-profile nature of OZ investments and significant tax benefits create enhanced audit risk. QOFs should maintain audit-ready documentation, engage experienced tax counsel, and implement robust compliance monitoring systems to defend tax positions.

Best Practices

Leading QOFs implement quarterly compliance reviews, maintain detailed asset tracking systems, engage specialist service providers, and establish clear operational procedures for ongoing compliance management. These practices minimize regulatory risk and protect investor benefits.
