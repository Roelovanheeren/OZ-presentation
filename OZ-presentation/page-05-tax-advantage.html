3. Investment Framework

Portfolio Construction Approach

Successful OZ investing requires sophisticated portfolio construction balancing tax optimization, risk management, and return generation. The framework emphasizes diversification across geography, asset classes, and investment stages while maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements.

Direct Investment

Self-Directed QOF: Maximum control and customization for sophisticated investors with specific market expertise and development capabilities.

Minimum Scale: Typically $10M+ for operational efficiency and regulatory compliance cost absorption.

Fund Investment

Institutional QOFs: Professional management, diversification, and operational leverage for investors seeking exposure without direct development risk.

Access Level: Minimums range from $250K to $10M+ depending on fund strategy and structure.

Risk-Return Optimization

The tax benefits create unique risk-return dynamics where tax alpha can justify accepting lower pre-tax returns for superior after-tax outcomes. The framework evaluates investments on an after-tax basis, incorporating the present value of deferred taxes and tax-free appreciation.

Tax Alpha Generation

Tax benefits can add 200-400 basis points annually to after-tax returns
Optimal structures maximize the present value of all three tax benefits
Long-term hold strategies generate maximum wealth creation

Implementation Strategy

The framework prioritizes investments in high-growth markets with strong demographic fundamentals, experienced operators with proven track records, and asset classes with multiple exit pathways. Geographic concentration in select markets provides operational efficiency while maintaining appropriate diversification.
